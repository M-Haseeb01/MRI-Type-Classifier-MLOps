Understanding brain tumors, MRI imaging, and AI-powered diagnosis

üß¨ Types of Brain Tumors

Glioma

Gliomas are tumors that originate from glial cells in the brain. They are the most common type of primary brain tumor.

  • Arise from supportive tissue of the brain
  • Can be slow-growing or aggressive
  • Include astrocytomas and oligodendrogliomas
  • Account for about 33% of all brain tumors

Meningioma

Meningiomas develop from the meninges, the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord.

  • Usually benign and slow-growing
  • Most common in adults aged 40-70
  • More frequent in women
  • Often treatable with surgery

Pituitary Tumor

Pituitary tumors form in the pituitary gland, which controls hormone production in the body.

  • Usually benign (non-cancerous)
  • Can affect hormone levels
  • May cause vision problems
  • Treatable with medication or surgery

üî¨ MRI Imaging Basics

What is MRI?

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is a non-invasive medical imaging technique that uses powerful magnets and radio waves to create detailed images of organs and tissues inside the body.

How MRI Works

  • Uses strong magnetic fields
  • Radio waves excite hydrogen atoms
  • Computer processes signals into images
  • Provides detailed soft tissue contrast
  • No radiation exposure

Why MRI for Brain Tumors?

  • Superior soft tissue visualization
  • Can detect small abnormalities
  • Shows tumor size and location
  • Helps distinguish tumor types
  • Monitors treatment progress

ü§ñ How Our AI Model Works

Deep Learning Architecture

Our model uses a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN), a type of deep learning architecture specifically designed for image analysis. The network has been trained on thousands of labeled MRI scans to learn patterns and features that distinguish different types of brain tumors.

1. Image Processing

  • MRI scan is uploaded
  • Image is resized to 224x224 pixels
  • Pixel values are normalized
  • Image is converted to RGB format

2. Feature Extraction

  • Convolutional layers detect patterns
  • Pooling layers reduce dimensions
  • Multiple layers learn hierarchical features
  • From edges to complex structures

3. Classification

  • Fully connected layers process features
  • Softmax activation for probabilities
  • 4 output classes (tumor types)
  • Confidence score for each class

Model Performance

Our model has been trained on a comprehensive dataset of brain MRI scans and achieves high accuracy in distinguishing between different tumor types. The model provides not just a single prediction, but probability scores for all classes, giving medical professionals additional context for their diagnosis.
